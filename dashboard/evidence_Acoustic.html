Acoustic Module (Voice-Based Indicators)

1. Informative Pauses

  • University of Miami: Informative pauses as MCI markers — Suggests pauses before content words reveal processing delay in MCI.
  • ScienceDirect: Pause patterns in dementia speech — Examines the nature of strategic pauses in ADRD speech.

2. Low Energy Levels

  • LIDSEN: Slow and flat speech in Alzheimer's — Links vocal energy decline to apathy and fatigue in AD patients.

3. Rhythm and Monotony

  • PMC: Acoustic features in cognitive impairment — Finds reduced rhythmic variation and pitch dynamics in MCI speech.

4. Unstable Speech

  • ScienceDirect: Acoustic indicators of instability — Identifies unstable vocal prosody as a key ADRD speech marker.

5. Vocal Fold Control

  • ScienceDirect: Laryngeal control deficits in Alzheimer's — Highlights reduced vocal fold tension and stability in dementia.

6. Tongue-Lip Coordination

  • LI Speech: Articulation changes with dementia — Discusses reduced fine motor control in speech articulation due to ADRD.
