Linguistic Module (Transcription-Based Indicators)

1. Frequent Repetition of Words and Phrases

  • Being Patient: Why People with Dementia Repeat Themselves — Explains how repetition in speech may indicate memory and word retrieval difficulties in dementia patients.
  • PMC: Verbal repetition in primary progressive aphasia — Investigates repetition and retrieval deficits across dementia subtypes.

2. Disfluencies and Filler Phrases

  • arXiv: Disfluency patterns in dementia speech — Highlights the correlation of "uh", "um", and hesitations with cognitive impairments.
  • Taylor & Francis: Linguistic cues in dementia — Discusses filler phrase frequency and cognitive load in ADRD patients.

3. Vague Terms and Semantic Clarity

  • ScienceDirect: Usage of vague terms by MCI patients — Shows increased use of indefinite terms (e.g., "thing", "it") in those at risk of cognitive impairment.

4. Coherent Narrative and Memory Recall

  • PMC: Story coherence as a marker for Alzheimer's — Demonstrates decreased narrative organization in early-stage AD.
  • PMC: Semantic control and discourse coherence — Links discourse disorganization to semantic processing challenges in dementia.
